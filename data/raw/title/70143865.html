Collectibles, relics and other prized possessions guide the Harrison family on a daily tour of history from behind their Las Vegas pawn shop counters.

  1. 1. Going, Going...Pawn!

    20m

    Rick gauges the authenticity of Babe Ruth's "called shot" bat and negotiates for a WWI surgical kit. Chum prices a pair of Salvador Dali sculptures.

  2. 2. Et tu, Rick?

    20m

    An ancient Roman coin and a rare WWII Alamo Scouts patch get Rick's history-buff blood pumping. Chum evaluates an original "Mad Max" movie poster.

  3. 3. Pawn or Bust

    20m

    The shop appraises unique artworks including a life-mask bust of actor Marlon Brando and a photographic print by Devo musician Mark Motherbaugh.

  4. 4. Chum's Risky Business

    20m

    Chum follows his instincts and buys seven boxes full of comic books, sight unseen. A first-edition pinball machine from 1871 impresses Rick.

  5. 5. E Equals MC Pawn

    20m

    Rick uses his math skills when a seller offers a hand-written equation from Albert Einstein. Chum buys a vintage headlight tester for restoration.

  6. 6. Wish-A-Pawn a Star

    20m

    A Disneyland fan offers the shop his well-preserved ride operator costumes from Disneyland's Pirates of the Caribbean and Haunted Mansion attractions.

  7. 7. Samurais and Centerfolds

    20m

    A photographer brings a "Playboy" stock certificate into the shop, while Rick marvels at ancient coins and a samurai sword owned by a feudal lord.

  8. 8. The Pawn Commandments

    20m

    Rick and Chum check out a rare automaton doll owned by a friend of Vegas star Murray the Magician. Rare book expert Rebecca assesses an antique Bible.

  9. 9. Royally Pawned

    20m

    Rick calls in some help to identify the origins of an impressive suit of English armor, but Chum buys a customized Gibson Les Paul guitar on his own.

  10. 10. Pawnie and Clyde

    20m

    The pawn stars consider purchasing a hand-written prison memoir from the sole surviving member of Bonnie and Clyde's infamous bank-robbing gang.

  11. 11. Top Rope, Bottom Dollar

    20m

    Rick admires the quality of a 17th-century ivory gunpowder flask, but he's skeptical about the origin of a half-dollar allegedly shot by Annie Oakley.

  12. 12. The Devil Went Down to Vegas

    20m

    The fine condition of a WWII folding paratrooper's bicycle awes Rick. A seller offers a fiddle signed by country music star Charlie Daniels.

  13. 13. Revolutionary Rick!

    20m

    In Washington, D.C., Rick considers buying a set of silver spoons made by Revolutionary War hero Paul Revere — plus another item of historical import.

  14. 14. Series Rings and Rap Kings

    20m

    Chum and Rick check out a BMW connected to a tragic episode in music history. The owner of a 2013 Boston Red Sox World Series ring wants to haggle.

  15. 15. Can't Pawn Me Love

    20m

    A collector gets in tune with Chum when he brings in 80 Beatles albums, including rarities. Rick negotiates for original Maurice Sendak illustrations.

  16. 16. Some Serious Coinage

    20m

    Rick sees dollar signs when two extremely rare coins arrive at the shop, while an antique "Jetsons" lunch box grabs Corey and Chum's attention.

  17. 17. Ultimate Fighting Pawn

    20m

    Chum sells one of Rick's favorite swords to UFC president Dana White. Rick sets his sights on a lovingly restored grand piano from the 1800s.

  18. 18. The Happiest Place to Pawn

    20m

    A speech signed by Martin Luther King Jr. inspires the gang, while a boat from Disney's "Jungle Cruise" has Rick and Chum ready to make a deal.

  19. 19. President's Day Sale

    20m

    A stunned Rick appraises a card signed by Abraham Lincoln. Corey and Chum take a shine to an item designed for President Bill Clinton.

  20. 20. Fully Vested

    20m

    Heads turn when a seller presents a pair of 18th-century breech-loading pistols. A unique garment worn by musician John Popper catches Chum's eye.

  21. 21. A Killer Cap

    20m

    Rick is mesmerized when the top hat and pistol belonging to an individual suspected of being Jack the Ripper lands on the counter.

  22. 22. Blades of Deal

    20m

    Rick sets his sights on owning a 1984 Ferrari 308, but can he close the deal? Corey falls hard for a phone-operated music player from the 1930s.

  23. 23. Pawned and Confused

    20m

    A rare stack of Led Zeppelin vinyls stirs interest in the shop. Rick hones in on a 19th-century desk and a piece of dirt-covered baseball memorabilia.

  24. 24. In the Presence of Greatness

    20m

    When a Batman utility-belt toy set from the 1960s shows up, Chum and Rick are ready to pounce. Rick's hunt for "The Object" continues.

  25. 25. Highly Explosive Pawn

    20m

    A prop from "Back to the Future Part II" leads to a quest for authentication. Rick feels his age when a creepy calendar arrives in the store.

  26. 26. Payne in the Putt

    20m

    Rick heads to the East Coast to take one of the first Jeeps ever manufactured for a spin and visits a bookstore to appraise an antique tome on alchemy.

  27. 27. Here's Looking at You, Pawn!

    20m

    An American first edition of "Peter Pan" glides into the shop, but Rick is more interested in a piece of the fallen rigid airship USS Shenandoah.

  28. 28. The Pistol is Mightier than the Sword

    20m

    The gang must assign values to a pistol sword from the 1700s, a portrait of theater actor John Barrymore and a 1969 Harley Davidson Aermacchi.

  29. 29. Big Fish in a Small Pawn

    20m

    Three San Francisco 49ers Super Bowl rings catch the attention of the appraisers, while Corey examines a four-barrel speargun.

  30. 30. A Treasure Remembered

    41m

    Through hilarious and touching clips from the past, the crew fondly remembers Richard Benjamin Harrison, aka "The Old Man," following his passing.
